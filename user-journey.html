Customer Journey Maps 3 Personas

Agentic Warranty Claims Engine -- POC Sprint

WA

Persona 1 -- Warranty Analyst

Daily, all-day queue work
"I need to know why it was flagged, not just that it was flagged."
PHASE
I
First-Pass Review
Daily -- ~2 min/claim
II
Deep Investigation
Per flagged claim -- ~3 min
III
Decision
~30 sec per action
IV
Escalation Return
Ad-hoc -- when assigned
V
Contest Resolution
Per contested claim -- ~5 min
Actions
What they do
1 Open queue -- 55 claims loaded, flagged sorted to top
2 Scan status badges: Clean / Flagged / Anomaly
3 Filter by tab or search by Claim ID / Dealer ID
4 Glance at savings counter for session context
5 Click flagged claim to open detail view
6 Read AI narrative -- 3 sentences, specific thresholds cited
7 Check per-rule PASS/FAIL breakdown with severity badges
8 Verify parts table -- costs, quantities, line totals
9 Click Approve -- claim marked green
10 Click Escalate -- flagged for senior review
11 Click Reject -- leakage counter increments
12 Reopen previously escalated claim
13 Review timeline -- see original decision context
14 Read updated AI brief if anything changed
15 Make final decision: approve or reject
16 Open contested claim -- customer pushed back
17 Read resolution brief: new evidence, re-validation, precedents
18 Enter ruling notes (required -- becomes customer explanation)
19 Uphold or overturn the original decision
Touchpoints
UI surfaces
 Claim Queue Status Badges Filter Tabs Savings Counter claims.json Claim Detail Metadata Panel AI Summary Panel Policy Flags Panel Parts Panel Action Bar Approve / Escalate / Reject Claim Detail Updated AI Brief Decision Timeline Resolution Brief Panel Re-validation Output Ruling Notes Field
Thinking
Internal thoughts
"Which claims need my attention most?"
"Let me start with the highest-amount flagged claims."
"8.5 labor hours vs. a 2.0-hour limit -- that's a clear reject."
"I would have missed the out-of-warranty mileage on my own."
"The AI recommendation matches my assessment."
"This borderline case needs a more senior set of eyes."
"I remember this one -- what's changed since I escalated it?"
"The updated brief makes the call straightforward now."
"The customer's diagnostic report actually changes the picture."
"My ruling notes need to be clear -- they'll go to the customer."
Feeling
Emotional state
Neutral -- orienting to today's queue
Impressed -- AI caught what I'd miss
Focused -- validating the reasoning
Confident -- specific data backs decision
Cautious -- need to recall original context
Careful -- ruling affects the customer
Assured -- precedents support the call
Pain Points & Opportunities
55-claim queue can feel overwhelming without clear priority signals
Pre-sorted by severity + amount surfaces highest-value claims first
Vague AI output erodes trust -- analyst reverts to manual review
Specific thresholds in the narrative ("325% overage") earn instant trust
Borderline claims cause hesitation without an escalate option
One-click actions remove friction; every decision is defensible
Returning to a claim without original context costs time
Decision timeline preserves full history
Ruling notes are mandatory but slow to write from scratch
Resolution brief pre-surfaces new evidence and precedents

CM

Persona 2 -- Claims Operations Manager

Low-medium -- monitoring & spot checks
"I don't need to open every claim. I need a number I can take upstairs."
PHASE
I
Morning Check
Daily -- ~2 min
II
Anomaly Investigation
Ad-hoc -- when patterns appear
III
Drill-In
Occasional -- ~3 min
IV
Reporting
Weekly/monthly -- ~5 min
Actions
What they do
1 Open app -- go straight to savings counter
2 Check leakage prevented total and accuracy %
3 Verify overnight batch processed without issues
4 Filter to Anomaly tab -- amber-badged claims
5 Spot dealer ID clusters in the anomaly list
6 Read AI pattern narrative -- statistical signals, not scores
7 Click into a specific anomaly claim for detail
8 Review AI summary explaining dealer behaviour pattern
9 Action directly or assign to an analyst
10 Pull savings counter metrics for leadership report
11 Compare anomaly detection rate vs. rules-only baseline
12 Track escalation rate and resolution time trends
Touchpoints
UI surfaces
 Savings Counter Accuracy % Claims Reviewed Claim Queue (Anomaly tab) AI Pattern Narrative Dealer ID Column Claim Detail AI Summary Panel Savings Counter Leakage Prevented ($)
Thinking
Internal thoughts
"Did the overnight batch process cleanly?"
"Accuracy is holding at 100% -- good."
"This dealer has 14 ECM claims in 30 days -- regional average is 2."
"These passed all rules but the agent still caught the pattern."
"The AI explains the statistical signal clearly. I can act on this."
"$12,450 leakage prevented -- that's a defensible number for leadership."
"Anomaly detection is catching what the old rules engine missed."
Feeling
Emotional state
Reassured -- metrics are healthy
Alert -- pattern demands attention
Validated -- system catching what rules missed
In control -- can act or delegate
Confident -- numbers are defensible
Pain Points & Opportunities
Leakage reports are currently retrospective -- damage already approved
Live counter provides real-time visibility without waiting for reports
Anomalies that don't trip rules pass through undetected
AI pattern detection catches statistical outliers beyond the rules engine
No aggregate view of dealer behaviour -- everything is claim-by-claim
Pattern narrative aggregates dealer-level signals in plain language
Metrics are manually assembled -- no live counter exists today
Dollar-value framing resonates directly with leadership

CU

Persona 3 -- The Customer

Rare -- per claim lifecycle
"I just need to understand why my claim was denied."
PHASE
I
Receive Decision
Day 1 -- notification
II
Understand Explanation
Day 1 -- ~2 min reading
III
Contest Decision
Day 1-3 -- if disagree
IV
Wait & Track
Days 3-10 -- async
V
Receive Resolution
Day 10+ -- final ruling
Actions
What they do
1 Receive notification that claim has been decided
2 Open customer portal to see the outcome
3 Read plain-language explanation of the decision
4 See specific numbers cited -- hours, mileage, thresholds
5 Decide: accept the outcome or push back
6 Click "I disagree with this decision"
7 Enter reason for contest
8 Attach supporting evidence (e.g. diagnostic report)
9 Submit -- receive immediate confirmation
10 Check status tracker -- 3-step progress indicator
11 See status advance from "Submitted" to "Under Review"
12 Receive resolution notification
13 Read plain-language ruling with explanation
14 See outcome: Upheld or Overturned
Touchpoints
Customer surfaces
 Email / SMS Notification Customer Portal Decision Explanation AI-Generated Customer Summary Contest Form "I Disagree" Button File Upload Contest Status Tracker 3-Step Progress Bar Resolution Notification Ruling Panel
Thinking
Internal thoughts
"I thought my vehicle was covered -- what happened?"
"8.5 labor hours vs. a 2.0-hour limit -- okay, I see the specific reason."
"But my situation was different -- I have a report that proves it."
"Is this actually going to be reviewed, or is this a dead end?"
"The form is simple -- I can upload my diagnostic report."
"Has anyone looked at my case yet?"
"Good -- the tracker moved to 'Under Review'."
"They overturned it -- the diagnostic report made the difference."
"Even if it wasn't overturned, at least I understand why."
Feeling
Emotional state
Anxious -- unexpected denial
Processing -- at least the reason is clear
Hopeful -- there is a real path to push back
Relieved -- submission confirmed immediately
Patient -- tracker shows progress
Respected -- the system took my case seriously
Pain Points & Opportunities
Denial feels arbitrary when the notification lacks detail
Notification links directly to a clear explanation page
Policy jargon ("R-10", "threshold violation") is meaningless to a customer
Plain language with specific numbers builds understanding and trust
Contest path feels designed to discourage if hard to find
"I disagree" button is visible, form is simple, confirmation is immediate
Silence after submission erodes trust -- "did anyone see this?"
3-step status tracker advances in real time
Rulings that arrive without explanation feel predetermined
Plain-language ruling -- even upheld -- explains what was considered
